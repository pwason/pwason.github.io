I wrote a critically acclaimed novel

 

From writing badly punctuated and terribly illustrated stories at the age of six, to crafting poems, to launching my very first novel, The Stormbringer, in February 2016, writing has played a huge part in defining me. This novel has been critically acclaimed by authors such as Shashi Tharoor, as well as Jeffrey Archer

 

      Writing has helped me collect my thoughts and coherently express myself

      Overcoming writers block and bouncing back from rejections by publishers taught me how to believe in myself and persevere, in spite of the odds

      Editing my book alongside a team of editors taught me how to remain patient and calm in the face of deadlines and stress

      Conducting myself at my book launch taught me how to be mature and confident in front of an audience, and connect with them on an emotional as well as intellectual level

 

      I can streamline my writing, and provide coherent, compelling arguments

      I have become more confident in professional scenarios, and am able to handle rejection better

      I use this poise and maturity to connect with my peers in daily life, as well as during seminars and classes that I teach

      I overcome roadblocks by approaching problems from different perspectives

      I have become more self-confident, and have used this to help my peers

I am the lead singer of a band

 

I have always loved to sing, but it was only in 2014 that I was fortunate enough to find a group of people as passionate about music as I am. My band, Bottlenook, has performed at several venues, both large and small, and continues to make its mark on the music scene of Mumbai. Most notably, we were the headliners at the Bombay Society for Prevention of Cruelty towards Animals Woofs and Hooves fundraiser held in January 2016.

      The ability to lead a team, both physically and emotionally

      That society can become an infinitely better place if we use our talents to work in its favour

 

      Overcome differences in different social situations, and producing stellar results

      Understand the importance of empathy and understanding in group scenarios

  • Discover the joy that comes with using artistic expression to catalyse social change

I am a classically trained Odissi dancer

 

I have been learning classical Odissi dance for over a decade, in an attempt to better understand my heritage and develop grace and poise. Of all the activities I have undertaken, this is the most therapeutic. The most notable experience yet is being awarded 3rd place in the National Odissi Exam, as well as performing as the protagonist, Kalki, in the performance of Dashavatar at the Vaishnovi Kalakshetra.

 

      Strenuous practises taught me the value of discipline, strength and handling situations with grace and poise

      Learning dance from my state of origin helped me connect with my roots

      The therapeutic nature of expression through the body

      The importance of getting into character, and interweaving emotions and the body

 

      I can handle any situation in a calm, controlled manner

      Invest myself in activities other than academics, hence further enriching myself

      I can better empathise with other perspectives in a situation

      I can throw myself into unfamiliar situations and still adapt

I helped marginalised children discover their voice

 

The summer of 2016, I engaged in what turned out to be the most meaningful activity I have undertaken as of yet. I volunteered at the St Jude India Childcare Centre as a teacher and a therapist to work with underprivileged children with cancer from all over India. I used the medium of story-telling to help them discuss their issues, and have curated an anthology of their stories and illustrations, Whispers of the Heart, to help raise awareness for their struggle, and the organisation that empowers them.

 

      I discovered the true power of love and compassion as a means to forge understanding and overcome differences

      I realised that helping the children made more of a difference to me than it did to them

      I learned the importance of community in times of hardship and sorrow

      I learned how to connect with different kinds of people, and bring out their full potential

      It has made me much more open-minded, and helped me form life-long bonds with people

      It made me more open to experiences and challenges, and has helped me mature as a person

      It has made me more receptive to the problems of my family and peers, and has given me the drive to help them overcome their issues

  • It has helped me understand the strengths and weaknesses of me and my peers, and helped me maximise our potential
